# FixIt v1 — Craftsman Editorial (Welcome screen)

**Настроение:** warm, honest, tactile. Дед-мастер в гараже с лампой. Workshop story as first-open.
**Палитра через природу:** aged oak workbench + linen cloth + soft amber lamp + pencil graphite
**X-instead-of-Y:** texture and warmth ARE the trust — material honesty replaces corporate polish

---

## Промпт

```
FixIt — AI home repair cost advisor. Photo of a broken thing — pipe, hinge, tile, outlet — returns a fair price to fix it yourself, split the work, or hire a pro. Real material prices, real labor rates, no surprises. For the moment you open the cupboard and see water where water should not be — that small panic before you know what it costs. Think Kinfolk magazine meets your grandfather's workshop — honest, lamp-lit, no rush.

Mood & visual identity: imagine a retired carpenter's workbench at the end of a good day — aged oak worn smooth by forty years of hands, a linen cloth folded beside a coffee-stained notebook, a soft amber lamp holding one warm circle of light over whatever problem you just brought in. This app lives in that lamp-light. The palette is materials, not colors — oiled oak warming into pale linen, pencil-graphite settling into iron-grey, a single saturated copper-amber for the lamp glow and the one action that matters. Warmth ARE the trust. Texture IS the interface.

The entire screen has a subtle linen-weave texture as its background — you can almost feel the canvas, like looking at a photograph of real fabric, with faint visible threads running both directions and the occasional pencil-grid ghost behind it. In the corners of the screen, small decorative flourishes hand-drawn in graphite — a tiny acorn glyph in the upper-right, a small plane-shaving curl in the lower-left, like someone's doodles in the margin of the notebook.

CRITICAL RULES:
1. If an element can be removed without losing warmth, remove it — but keep the warmth at any cost.
2. All shapes are soft organic curves, rounded rectangles, and hand-drawn lines — nothing machine-perfect, nothing sharp except the one copper-amber call-to-action.
3. One single accent color — copper-amber — appears ONLY on the lamp's glow and the primary CTA pill. Everything else is graphite, linen, oak, bone.
4. Typography is bookish — a warm serif for the hero headline (like a chapter title in a 1975 repair manual), a thin sans for body copy, hand-like italic for small margin annotations. Generous letter-spacing on UPPERCASE TRACKED labels.
5. The overall feeling is slow — every illustration should feel hand-drawn, every edge should feel pencil-sketched, nothing should feel stock-iconified.

Large hero illustration occupying the upper two-thirds of the screen: a richly-drawn scene of an aged-oak workbench seen from slightly above — the grain of the wood visible, knots and dings and one faint coffee ring on the left side. In the center of the workbench, a single warm amber lamp at a slight angle, its shade casting a soft warm circle of light that glows out into the surrounding oak. Inside the lamp's circle of light: a small linen-wrapped bundle holding a leaky faucet cartridge, water-drop-shaped glyphs rising gently from it like steam from a teacup — three or four small droplets drawn in light graphite, suggesting slow motion. Scattered around the workbench at the edges of the lamp-light, hand-drawn sketches of three other problem-items fading into the darker oak: a loose door hinge in the upper-left corner, a cracked ceramic tile in the lower-right, a broken chair-leg in the lower-left — each drawn smaller and fainter, like they're waiting their turn under the lamp. A linen cloth folded neatly to the right of the lamp, a coffee-stained notebook open to its right with a pencil resting on it. The entire scene is lit from the lamp, warm falloff at the edges into deeper oak.

Below the hero illustration, centered in the lower third of the screen:

A UPPERCASE TRACKED label in muted graphite: "FIXIT · HOME REPAIR ADVISOR"

Below the label, a hero headline in warm serif italic-mixed-with-roman, two lines:
"Snap a photo."
"Know what it costs."

A single line of body text beneath in pencil-grey: "Real prices. Real options. No guesswork."

Below the body, a single copper-amber pill button, crisp-edged and saturated, holding the weight of the entire screen — the lamp made into an action: "TAKE A PHOTO OF YOUR PROBLEM" in clean medium sans, letter-spaced. The button feels like something you'd pick up with your hand.

Below the button, a small whisper-weight link in graphite: "or try a sample problem →" with the arrow hand-drawn.

Near the bottom edge, a decorative horizontal row of four tiny Polaroid-style thumbnails paperclipped to the linen — each Polaroid shows a different problem-sketch drawn in graphite: a water faucet, a cracked tile, a door hinge, a dead appliance. They're slightly tilted at different angles, like reference cards someone pinned up for you. Each Polaroid has a hand-drawn caption beneath: "plumbing · tiles · hinges · appliances" in small italic hand-like script.

At the very base, three tiny decorative graphite marks drawn freehand — a small circle, a small square, a small triangle — acting as a minimalist progress indicator for the onboarding flow. The first one is softly filled copper-amber (you are here).

No navigation bar at this point — this is before the app opens. Just the workbench and the lamp and the pencil marks.

Motion-directive: the lamp should feel like it WANTS to flicker softly; the water drops from the cartridge should feel like they WANT to fall slowly; the Polaroids should feel like they WANT to rustle in a faint breeze. Static image, but full of suggested breath.

Primary Design Surface: App.
```

## Что должно выйти (ожидание)

- Full-screen linen-weave pattern behind everything (texture visible)
- Upper 2/3 — painted workbench scene with amber lamp casting warm circle
- Faucet cartridge inside lamp-light, water drops drawn in graphite rising like steam
- Three faded problem-sketches at edges (hinge, tile, chair)
- Decorative graphite flourishes in corners (acorn, plane-shaving curl)
- Copper-amber CTA pill — ONLY saturated color
- Warm serif hero "Snap a photo. / Know what it costs."
- Row of 4 tilted Polaroid thumbnails with handwritten captions
- Overall hand-drawn, pencil-sketched, no stock icons

## Против чего тестировать

- Не должно выйти "Etsy wedding invite" — должна быть workshop authority, не crafting
- Не должно выйти "hipster coffee shop" — лампа + workbench = мастер, не barista
- Должно выйти "grandfather's trusted hands" — warm authority with real weight
