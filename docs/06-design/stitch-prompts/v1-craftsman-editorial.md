# FixIt v1 — Craftsman Editorial

**Настроение:** warm, honest, tactile. Дед в гараже который объяснит спокойно.
**Палитра через природу:** aged oak workbench + linen cloth + soft amber lamp + pencil graphite
**X-instead-of-Y:** texture and warmth ARE the trust — material honesty replaces corporate polish

---

## Промпт

```
FixIt — AI home repair cost advisor. Photo of a broken thing — pipe, hinge, tile, outlet — returns a fair price to fix it yourself, split the work, or hire a pro. Real material prices, real labor rates, no surprises. For the moment you open the cupboard and see water where water should not be — that small panic before you know what it costs.

Mood & visual identity: imagine a retired carpenter's workbench at the end of a good day — aged oak worn smooth by forty years of hands, a linen cloth folded beside a coffee-stained notebook, a soft amber lamp holding one warm circle of light over three options laid out in honest rows. This app lives in that lamp-light — the moment someone reliable looks at your problem and says "this is what it actually costs." The palette is materials, not colors — oiled oak warming into pale linen, pencil-graphite settling into iron-grey, a single saturated copper-amber for the recommended choice. Warmth ARE the trust. Texture IS the interface.

Three options rest on the page like tools laid out on the workbench — the cheapest on the left where you'd reach first, the middle a compromise, the full-service on the right where the heavy tools live. Each option is a card of soft linen-textured surface, each with its own quiet presence. The recommended option glows copper-amber like the lamp found it first.

Functional aesthetic — color temperature shows effort, not risk. Cool graphite for DIY (minimal warmth, minimal cost). Warm linen for Hybrid (mixed work, mixed warmth). Saturated copper-amber for Pro (full light, full service). Price scale and effort scale move together through color.

The anchor: the single recommended card glows a saturated copper-amber, outlined in a clean sharp edge that says "I am the answer. Pick me up." Everything else sits in quiet graphite and linen, waiting. The recommended card has a definite shape while the others have soft gradient edges.

Typography is bookish and calm. A serif for the diagnosis headline ("Leaky Kitchen Faucet Cartridge" reads like a chapter title in a repair manual from 1975). Thin sans for prices and labor hours. Numbers are the heroes — "$15", "$45", "$180", "30 min", "2 hours", "half-day" — each price sits at the surface of the linen, casting a faint pencil-shadow, clear and honest.

One screen. The workbench under the lamp.

A small circular thumbnail of the user's photo in the upper-left, rendered like a Polaroid pinned to a corkboard. Beside it, a UPPERCASE TRACKED label in muted graphite — "WHAT WE FOUND" — and below in warm serif: "Leaky Kitchen Faucet Cartridge." A single line of body text beneath in pencil-grey: "Worn rubber O-ring. Common failure, simple fix."

Below the diagnosis, a UPPERCASE TRACKED section label: "YOUR THREE ROUTES."

Three option-cards in a horizontal row (responsive: stacked on phone), each linen-textured with its own corner folded like a dog-eared page.

[DIY card — cool graphite tone, hollow] Top: a small crossed-hammer-and-wrench glyph drawn like a sketch. Middle: "DIY" in clean serif + "$12 – $18" in large thin serif below. Underneath in pencil-grey body: "30 minutes. O-ring kit from Home Depot. Instructions included." At the bottom corner: "Save $165 vs pro" in tiny UPPERCASE TRACKED muted green.

[Hybrid card — warm linen tone, filled slightly] Top: a small handshake glyph sketched. Middle: "Hybrid" in clean serif + "$40 – $55". Body: "You buy the cartridge, a plumber installs in 45 min. Good if you want the part chosen right."

[Pro card — the anchor, saturated copper-amber glow, hard-edged, definite] Top: a small toolbox glyph drawn with a confident line. Middle: "Full Pro" in clean serif + "$150 – $210" in large. Body: "Licensed plumber, 2-hour visit, 1-year warranty. Schedule same-day." At the card's base, a small copper-amber pill button: "FIND A PRO."

Below the three cards, a quiet horizontal line of pencil graphite.

Beneath the line, a small savings headline in serif: "Fix it yourself — save $165." Then a single soft-graphite outlined pill button: "START DIY GUIDE."

At the very bottom edge, a whisper-thin navigation with four small line-drawn icons (home, history, saved, profile), like pencil marks on the margin of the page — never the focus.

Primary Design Surface: App.
```

---

## Что должно выйти (ожидание)

- Оак-линен палитра, warm and inviting
- Три option-cards как tools on a workbench, copper-amber Pro card светится
- Serif для headlines (magazine-like, repair-manual-from-1975 feel)
- Thin sans для digits ($15, $45, $180)
- Photo thumbnail как Polaroid pinned to corkboard
- Pencil-drawn glyphs для tools (не filled icons из SF Symbols)
- Horizontal divider lines как графитные штрихи

## Против чего тестировать

- Не должно выйти "Nike editorial fitness" — слишком sporty
- Не должно выйти "Apple minimalism" — слишком cold
- Должно выйти "репетитор объясняет спокойно" — warm authority
